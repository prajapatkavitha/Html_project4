malaikofta Recipe

Ingredients :

    For Kofta:

    • 2 large Potatoes, boiled and mashed
    • 1 cup Paneer (Indian cottage cheese), grated
    • 2 tablespoons Cornflour
    • Salt, to taste
    • 1/2 teaspoon Red Chili Powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon Garam Masala
    • 2 tablespoons Raisins
    • 2 tablespoons Cashew Nuts, chopped
    • Oil, for frying

    For Gravy:

    • 2 large Onions, finely chopped
    • 3 medium Tomatoes, pureed
    • 1 tablespoon Ginger-Garlic Paste
    • 2 Green Chilies, chopped
    • 1 teaspoon Cumin Seeds
    • 1 Bay Leaf
    • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric Powder
    • 1 teaspoon Red Chili Powder
    • 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder
    • 1 teaspoon Garam Masala
    • 1/2 cup Cream
    • 10-12 Cashew Nuts, soaked and ground to a paste
    • Salt, to taste
    • 2 tablespoons Oil
    • Water, as needed
    • Fresh Coriander Leaves, for garnish

Preparation Process :

Kofta:

  1. Prepare Kofta Mixture: In a bowl, combine boiled and mashed potatoes,
    grated paneer, cornflour, salt, red chili powder, and garam masala. Mix well.
    Take a small portion of the mixture, flatten it, and place a few raisins
    and chopped cashew nuts in the center. Roll into balls.
  2. Fry Koftas: Heat oil in a deep frying pan.
    Fry the koftas until golden brown.
    Drain on paper towels and set aside.

Gravy:

  1. Prepare Masala: Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and bay leaf.
    Sauté for a few seconds. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
    Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  2. Add Spices: Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
    Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  3. Finish Gravy: Add cashew nut paste and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
    Add water to achieve the desired consistency and bring to a boil.
    Add cream and garam masala. Simmer for a few minutes.
    Adjust salt and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  4. Combine and Serve: Add the koftas to the gravy just before serving.
    Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
